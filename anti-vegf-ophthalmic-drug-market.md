<p><p>The Anti-VEGF ophthalmic drug market comprises several key drugs targeting vascular endothelial growth factor to treat retinal diseases. Pegaptanib is a pegylated RNA aptamer used primarily for wet age-related macular degeneration (AMD). Ranibizumab, a monoclonal antibody fragment, is also indicated for wet AMD and other conditions. Aflibercept, a fusion protein, offers a broader application, while Brolucizumab, a newer entrant, aims for extended dosing intervals. Faricimab is unique as it targets both VEGF and angiopoietin-2, addressing various aspects of ocular diseases.</p></p>
