<p><p>The artificial sweetener market includes several key types: Aspartame, Acesulfame-K, Monosodium Glutamate (MSG), Saccharin, and Sodium Benzoate. Aspartame is widely used in low-calorie products, while Acesulfame-K offers a sugar-like taste without calories. MSG enhances flavor in savory foods, but is not a sweetener. Saccharin, one of the oldest sweeteners, is often found in diet foods. Sodium Benzoate, though primarily a preservative, is used in beverages. Each type serves various dietary and culinary needs within the market.</p></p>
