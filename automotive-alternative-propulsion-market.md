<p><p>The automotive alternative propulsion market includes three main types: Fuel Cell Electric Vehicles (FCEVs), Hybrid Electric Vehicles (HEVs), and Battery Electric Vehicles (EVs). FCEVs use hydrogen fuel cells to generate electricity for propulsion, producing only water as a byproduct. HEVs combine traditional internal combustion engines with electric motors, improving fuel efficiency while reducing emissions. EVs rely solely on electric power stored in batteries, producing zero tailpipe emissions and offering a cleaner alternative to conventional vehicles. Each type contributes to a sustainable automotive future.</p></p>
