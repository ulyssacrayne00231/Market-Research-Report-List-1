<p><p>The infrared carbon dioxide sensor market primarily includes two types: Non-Dispersive Infrared (NDIR) sensors and Distributed Feedback Laser (DFB) sensors. NDIR sensors work by measuring the absorption of infrared light at specific wavelengths, making them effective for detecting CO2 concentrations. They are widely used in environmental monitoring and HVAC systems. In contrast, DFB sensors utilize laser technology for high sensitivity and accuracy, making them suitable for industrial applications and research settings where precise measurements are critical.</p></p>
