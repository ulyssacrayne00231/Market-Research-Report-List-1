<p><p>Molten Carbonate Fuel Cells (MCFC) are a type of high-temperature fuel cell technology that operates at temperatures around 600 to 700 degrees Celsius. They utilize a molten carbonate salt mixture as the electrolyte, allowing for efficient electrochemical reactions that convert fuels, typically natural gas or hydrogen, into electricity. MCFCs are known for their high efficiency, ability to operate on various fuels, and suitability for large-scale power generation and industrial applications.</p><p>The MCFC market is anticipated to grow at a CAGR of 6.8% during the forecast period, driven by rising demand for clean and efficient energy solutions to address global energy challenges. Moreover, advancements in technology aimed at improving the longevity and performance of MCFC systems are enhancing their appeal. The shift towards decarbonization and the integration of MCFCs in microgrid applications also contribute to market expansion.
