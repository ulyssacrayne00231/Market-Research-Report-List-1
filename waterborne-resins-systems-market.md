<p><p>Waterborne resin systems are categorized primarily into high molecular weight and low molecular weight types. High molecular weight resins offer enhanced mechanical properties, durability, and film-forming capabilities, making them suitable for applications in coatings and adhesives. In contrast, low molecular weight resins provide faster drying times and improved flexibility, often used in electronics and automotive components. Both types are designed to be environmentally friendly, reducing volatile organic compound emissions while maintaining performance across various industrial applications.</p></p>
